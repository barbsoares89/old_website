A proud Latinx immigrant, she writes genre-driven fare from the perspective of the outsider. image: images/wall_headshot.jpeg - title: text: "About" desc: | Bárbara Soares was born and raised in Brazil, spent a few years in the UK as a teenager, and made the move to America on her own at 18 to “pursue the dream.” She is an alum of the Florida State University Film School and The UCLA MFA Screenwriting program and is currently a staff writer on the Apple+ show Ghostwriter.

Bárbara works in the genre space and loves everything sci-fi, mystery, and supernatural. Her stories always feature strong and diverse female leads and usually tackle questions of identity, culture, and (dis)connection.

Growing up in three wildly different countries was the inspiration for much of Bárbara’s work. In Brazil, she wasn’t your standard Brazilian: she couldn’t get a tan to save her life and thought Carnaval was too loud. In England, she was the foreigner who had learned American English and was teased when she called a “rubber” an eraser; in Florida, she was the Latino who didn’t know what a taco was and who watched lots of obscure foreign films but had never seen “E.T.” In short, she never fit into a mold and never really wanted to.

Bárbara’s favorite filmmaker is Stanley Kubrick (as you can probably tell from this website).
